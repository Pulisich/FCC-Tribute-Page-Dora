Dora Angelina Guerrieri

Greatest Teacher I have ever known.

Here are some interesting facts of her life:

  • She was born in 1939 in Córdoba, Argentina.
  • Her father Américo Guerrieri was a World War I hero fighting against the Austro Hungarian Army in the Italian Alps.
  • She became a teacher at 24 and taught in Dean Funes School until she retired as Director.
  • She married Alberto Enrique Pulisich in 1960. Which is ironic since Pulisich is an Austro Hungarian last name.
  • She gave birth to Cecilia, Pablo, and Federico, in 1961, 1963, and 1967.
  • During 2010 to her death in 2018 she was elected president of her local branch of the Rotary Club
  • During her time there were several happenings she survived: WWII, the Peronist coup, the Revolución Libertadora, the return of Perón, the Last Dictatorship of Argentina, the Falklands/Malvinas War, the fall of the Dictatorship, the fall of the USSR, the AMIA bombings, the Rio III explosion, the 9/11 attacks, the great Argentinian recession of 2001.
  • She knew how to play the piano and had one on her living room.
  • She was a devoted Catholic who truly upheld Christianity's values of tolerance, compassion, and peace.
  • She passed away on March 25th of 2018 after a short battle with liver cancer derived from Hepatitis C, which she contracted by accidental transfusion in 1994.
  • She was beloved by her grandsons María, Eliseo, Tomás, Pablo, Josefina, and Guillermina.
